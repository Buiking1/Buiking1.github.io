About ABRAHAM OKEWU FOUNDATION

ABRAHAM OKEWU FOUNDATION was established to respond to the growing gap between the potential of Nigerian youth and the opportunities available to them. Recognizing that many talented young people are held back by limited access to education, skills training, and guidance, the foundation exists to bridge that gap in a practical and sustainable way.

We work closely with schools, communities, faith-based groups, and local partners to identify young men and women who are eager to learn but lack resources. Through targeted support—scholarships, vocational training, mentorship, and learning hubs—we help them take meaningful steps toward a better future for themselves and their communities.

Vision Statement

To see a Nigeria where every young person is equipped with the knowledge, skills, and opportunities needed to realize their full potential, lead self-sufficient lives, and drive positive, sustainable change in their communities.

How We Work

Our programs are structured to address the full journey of a young person—from access to education to skills, mentorship, and community support.

Educational Sponsorship

Removing financial barriers to learning

We partner with schools and communities to identify bright students who are at risk of dropping out due to financial hardship. Our sponsorship covers core educational needs and ongoing mentoring support.

  • Tuition, examination, and registration fees
  • School supplies, uniforms, and learning materials
  • Academic counselling and performance tracking
  • Parental engagement and support programs

Leadership & Mentorship

Guiding young people toward purpose and impact

Through structured mentoring relationships, leadership workshops, and peer learning, we help young people discover their strengths and build the confidence to lead in their communities.

  • Mentor matching with professionals and community leaders
  • Leadership, ethics, and civic responsibility trainings
  • Youth-led community service and advocacy initiatives

Vocational & Entrepreneurship Development

Turning skills into sustainable livelihoods

Our trainings are designed with local market realities in mind. We combine hands-on learning with basic financial literacy and business development support.

  • Technical and trade-based skills (e.g., tailoring, carpentry)
  • Digital literacy, basic coding, and online work readiness
  • Entrepreneurship bootcamps and small grants (where available)
  • Partnerships with employers for internships and placements

Learning Hubs & Community Outreach

Safe, equipped spaces for growth and collaboration

Our hubs act as local anchors where young people can study, access the internet, receive tutoring, and participate in workshops on life skills, health, and personal development.

  • Community libraries and reading clubs
  • Access to computers, educational software, and the internet
  • Health, wellbeing, and life skills programs
